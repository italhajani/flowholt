# Explore Make Grid GUI

13 min

﻿Make﻿ Grid comes with easy to use navigation and interaction tools to view and customize the canvas.

## Make Grid navigation and interaction tools

![Navigate and interact with Make Grid](https://images.archbee.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/Ng5HHPdepvXZ3mEekql_j-20260317-110417.png?format=webp "Navigate and interact with Make Grid")

﻿

### 1. Search

Using the **Find anything** feature, you can search for any entity such as a table column, URL, or an app, Make﻿ Grid will immediately display the relevant scenarios﻿ and their dependencies.

### 2. Filter

You can narrow down your search results by scenario﻿ properties such as status, folder, apps being used, creator, etc.

### 3. **Need your attention**

You can view errors due to exceeded data store capacity, queued items in a webhook, deactivation or incomplete executions here. By clicking on the errors displayed, you can immediately locate where the error lies and resolve the issue.

### 4. **Islands**

Each island represents a folder in your Make﻿ organization. When you zoom into an island, you can see the dependencies and scenarios﻿ that make up the island.

### 5. Presence widget

You can see active team members and can see each other in real time. Active teammates are visible via live cursors and a presence widget in the toolbar, making it easier to collaborate.

![Document image](https://images.archbee.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/gh8sG92X_jOzNaCudVwjA-20260319-113026.png?format=webp "Document image")

﻿

Toggle on and off the **Show Cursors** buttonto enable or disable the cursors.

### 6. **User support and feedback**

You can submit feedbackusing the **Give Feedback** feature. Clicking the **Resources** icon will take you to the help resources of Make﻿ Grid. You can also view your organization and user details from the additional menu options.

### 7. **Modes, views, and zoom**

**Dashboard mode**: In this mode, you can view the important metrics and data at a glance.

**2D/3D view**: You can switch between two and three dimensional views by toggling between **2D/3D** button.

**Dark/Light mode**: Click the sun or moon icon to switch between light and dark visual themes.

**Z****oom:** Use the **+/-** to zoom in and out of the Grid. Alternatively, scroll up/down or pinch on the trackpad to zoom.

**Last update**: The cloud icon displays the time elapsed since the last data synchronization, so you can always tell how current your data is at a glance.

### 8. **Edit Layout**

With the **Edit Layout**, you can customize your workspace layout to suit your workflow. Use the **Edit Layout** button in the toolbar to enter edit mode, where you can freely resize assets, folders (islands), or teams by dragging them into position. Use the yellow resize handles to adjust the size of folders and teams as needed.

![Document image](https://archbee-image-uploads.s3.amazonaws.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/CiwEB-84PPtaoyL95BcBx-20260316-121738.gif "Document image")

﻿

Use the **Compact folder layout** to rearrange assets and remove empty spaces. This action cannot be undone.

![Document image](https://images.archbee.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/9DbnwphuXIeKVgTPbWftl-20260317-111859.png?format=webp "Document image")

﻿

### 9. **Select Layer and** Take snapshot

With the **Select Layer** feature, you can select between different views on your automation landscape, such as:

* **Explore**: Use this layer to view your core assets and the direct connections between them.

* **Credits**: Use this layer to view the credits used in the last 30 days for scenarios, folders, and teams.

* **Data Transfer**: Use this layer to view the data transferred in the last 30 days for scenarios, folders, and teams.

* **Operations**: Use this layer to view the operations completed in the last 30 days for scenarios, folders, and teams.

Use the **What needs attention** toggle to show or hide the **Need your attention** widget.

![Document image](https://images.archbee.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/MfQRCMdmf_E6A_-URR8AL-20260317-111158.png?format=webp "Document image")

﻿

With the **Take snapshot** feature you can take snapshots of your grid. The image is saved in your downloads automatically.

### 10. **Team**

A group of people working on a collection of related scenarios﻿ in Make﻿. A team can also be called a collection of islands. You can work together with your team and see active teammates and their cursors in real-time.

### 11. **Usage status**

This feature is only displayed when a layer is selected. It serves as a quick performance indicator, helping you monitor credits used, data transferred, and operations consumed in the last 30 days.

﻿

## Make Grid elements

Use the following guide to interpret the specific elements that populate your Make﻿ Grid. These markers represent some of the core building blocks of your automation landscape:

**Scenarios**

![Document image](https://images.archbee.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/tjKkQY7ACmhJQT2VH9XlC-20251209-183858.png?format=webp "Document image")

﻿

This icon represents a **Scenario**. In Make﻿, a scenario﻿ is a visual workflow that automates how data flows and actions are executed between connected applications and services.

**Webhooks**

![Document image](https://images.archbee.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/RlBi5W2vKW2Txm0E_I7DL-20251209-184251.png?format=webp "Document image")

﻿

This icon indicates the presence or configuration of a **Webhook** that facilitates event-driven integration with external services or applications. The color of the Webhook may vary depending on the application it is connected to.

**Assets**

![Document image](https://images.archbee.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/7o96YSdm5K0md61ESlTa_-20251209-184936.png?format=webp "Document image")

﻿

This stacked square icon represents an **Asset**. The icon may vary depending on the type of asset you select. It represents a data storage element such as Google Sheets, Airtable, or a database.

**Generic objects**

![Document image](https://images.archbee.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/C_me4gEwuKfFOzqrGWC11-20251209-185528.png?format=webp "Document image")

﻿

This hexagonal icon represents a generic object. It can refer to items such as lists, AI assistants, mailboxes, channels, forms, or services. In Make﻿ Grid, only a limited set of applications is displayed explicitly; all other applications are grouped under services.

**Agent**

![Document image](https://images.archbee.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/JOiClCM7p061bsylUwZwR-20251209-192827.png?format=webp "Document image")

﻿

This icon represents the presence of an **AI Agent**.

**Shared assets**

![Document image](https://images.archbee.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/uRVe5QOHwoL_Zh39U6eBY-20251209-192213.png?format=webp "Document image")

﻿

**Shared asset** indicates that the module is shared across folders or used in multiple scenarios﻿.

**Links and arrows**

![Document image](https://images.archbee.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/0m-JoH1kw_hI9KVVxPFBX-20251209-193315.png?format=webp "Document image")

﻿

Purple links with an arrow are trigger links, they explain how one module triggers another. The violet links are data links that show how a module interacts with data from another module.
