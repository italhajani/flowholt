# Scenario templates

5 min

Templates are ready-to-use scenarios that help you get started quickly with Make﻿. Think of them as pre-set workflows that you can:

* Use immediately as complete scenarios

* Edit and expand to fit your specific use case

* Share with others in your team or the wider Make﻿ community

* Save and reuse later

Whether you need a simple automation or a complex workflow, templates give the foundation to build exactly what you need.

Templates are available on all [Make plans](https://www.make.com/en/pricing "Make plans"). Anyone can create and share scenario templates.

## Types of scenario templates

﻿Make﻿ offers two types of scenario templates:

* **Public templates** are those created by Make﻿ and its user community, and cover over 7,500 use cases. They are available to all Make﻿ users.

* **Team templates** arethosecreated by you and your team members. They are available to your team and can be shared with users outside of the team via a public link.

## Where to find scenario templates

To view all available scenario templates, click the **Templates** tab in the left sidebar. There, you can see both public and team templates:

## What you can do with scenario templates

In Make﻿, you can:

* Create a scenario from a template

* Create new templates

* Edit existing templates

* Delete templates

* Publish templates

* Share templates

* Submit templates to the public templates library

Learn below how to **create a scenario from a template**, or explore further how to [**create and manage your own templates**](https://help.make.com/create-and-manage-scenario-templates "create and manage your own templates")**.**

## Create a scenario from a template

You can create scenarios using public or team templates.

### Using a public template

To create a scenario using a public template:

1

Click **Templates** in the left sidebar.

2

Open the **Public templates** tab**.**

3

Select the template from the gallery, or use the search box to find a specific one by app name or an action you want to perform.

![Document image](https://images.archbee.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/W6kmdTTeD6x0EmdO6gsuG-20251001-155413.png?format=webp "Document image")

﻿

4

Choose one of the following options:

**Option 1: Start guided setup**

This option includes on-screen prompts to help you configure the template.

* Click **Start guided setup**.

![Document image](https://images.archbee.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/ZOPjLz1lG6QbolVzFnW3B-20251001-155535.png?format=webp "Document image")

﻿

* Configure the module settings following the prompts for each field, or click **Skip guided setup** at any time to continue without prompts.

![Document image](https://images.archbee.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/YJUw4-JxKO7W13cRRbqY7-20251001-155650.png?format=webp "Document image")

﻿

* While configuring modules with the guided setup, you'll see different icons showing the current status of the module:

![Document image](https://images.archbee.com/PL8X94efBsjvhfQV3wyyj-8xM3nFM-Soo2Hd2eeZ38L-20250625-073734.png?format=webp "Document image")

﻿

* A **checkmark** indicates that you've filled every field correctly and saved the changes.

* A **black star** indicates that you've opened the module and filled every field correctly, but haven't saved the changes.

* An **exclamation point** indicates that some field has an invalid input or something is missing.

* A **grey star** indicates that you haven't opened the module yet.

**Option 2: Create new scenario from template**

With thisoption, you create a new scenario and configure manually all the module settings, including **Connections**, without any guided assistance.

* Click **Create new scenario from template**.

![Document image](https://app.archbee.com/api/optimize/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/pZSkoakcLTVTllYaihLmX-20251001-155755.png "Document image")

﻿

* Edit your new scenario based on your workflow the same way you would edit any other scenario.

After creating a new scenario from a public template, you can rename it, save, test and schedule as needed.

### Using a team template

To create a scenario using a team template:

1

Click **Templates** in the left sidebar.

2

Switch to the **Team templates** tab**.**

3

Select the template you want to use.

![Document image](https://images.archbee.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/hRGUDs7eHnCoGqO2XlEb2-20251001-160343.png?format=webp "Document image")

﻿

4

Click **Options**, and then **+ Create scenario**.

![Document image](https://app.archbee.com/api/optimize/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/fpu_sGGnJLGtnmr4lqDeX-20251001-160412.png "Document image")

﻿

You can now edit, schedule, and run the scenario.
