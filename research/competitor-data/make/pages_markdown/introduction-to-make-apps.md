# Introduction to Make apps

5 min

﻿Make﻿ is a no-code integration platform that allows you to connect multiple apps and facilitate data transfer between them. By using the modules within these apps, you can design scenarios﻿ that can be scheduled to align with your workflows.

One of the key benefits of Make﻿ is that it empowers you to share data across apps without requiring any coding expertise. To read more about how Make﻿ works, go to our [Get started](https://help.make.com/get-started "Get started") section.

## What are Make﻿ apps?

﻿Make﻿ apps are integrations of third-party APIs or Make﻿ built-in tools. Each app consists of [modules](/types-of-modules)﻿.

## Types of Make﻿ apps

﻿Make﻿ divides apps into different types based on their origin and availability:

* **V****erified apps**: apps verified by Make﻿ developers

* **C****ommunity apps**: apps developed by partners and power users

* **C****ustom apps**: apps developed by Make﻿ users

## Verified apps

Verified apps are developed either by Make﻿ developers or by other Make﻿ users. Verified apps made by a third party have to pass the Make﻿ [app review process](https://developers.make.com/custom-apps-documentation/app-review "app review process") and have to be actively maintained.

Verified apps further differ in their availability in Make﻿ [pricing plans](https://www.make.com/en/pricing "pricing plans"):

* **S****tandard apps**: All Make﻿ users can use standard apps.

* **P****aid plan apps**: All Make﻿ users with a paid plan (Core, Pro, Teams, and Enterprise) can use paid plan apps.

* **E****nterprise apps**: All Make﻿ users in organizations with the Enterprise plan can use enterprise apps.

## Community apps

Community apps are developed by Make﻿ partners and power users who have taken dedicated training. Unlike verified apps, community apps don't go through Make﻿'s app review process. Support, maintenance, and monetization of a community app is the responsibility of the app developer.

You can check all the community apps on the [Make Apps & services page](https://www.make.com/en/integrations?community=1&verified=1 "Make Apps & services page") when you filter to show community apps only.

In the Scenario﻿ Builder, you can identify the community apps by the **Community** label:

![Community app example](https://app.archbee.com/api/optimize/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/HWxzUOnu9-zSJyV2RhkIt_image.png "Community app example")

﻿

Community apps may require an additional purchase to use. Make﻿ does not earn money or commission on these purchases. You purchase them directly from the developer.

To use a community app in your scenarios﻿, you must first install the app in your Make﻿ organization. You must have the **Admin**, **Owner**, or **App Developer** role to install an app.

You can learn more about Community apps and how to create them in our Developer Hub:

* ﻿[About Community Apps](https://developers.make.com/custom-apps-documentation/community-apps/about "About Community Apps")﻿

* ﻿[Monetization, pricing, and billing](https://developers.make.com/custom-apps-documentation/community-apps/how-does-it-work#monetization-pricing-and-billing "Monetization, pricing, and billing")﻿

* ﻿[App support](https://developers.make.com/custom-apps-documentation/community-apps/how-does-it-work#app-support "App support")﻿

## Custom apps

Custom apps are developed by Make﻿ users. Make﻿ provides a [web interface](https://developers.make.com/custom-apps-documentation/get-started/make-apps-editor/web-editor-features "web interface") where you can create and manage your custom app configurations.

Custom apps are available to Make﻿ users based on their [visibility](https://developers.make.com/custom-apps-documentation/app-visibility "visibility"):

* **P****rivate apps**: The custom app developer and users in all organizations they are part of can use the app.

* **P****ublic apps**: Access to the app is on an invite-only basis. The app developer has to share an invite link with you.

To learn more about creating custom apps, check the [Custom Apps documentation](https://developers.make.com/custom-apps-documentation "Custom Apps documentation").
