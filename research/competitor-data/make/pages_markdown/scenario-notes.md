# Scenario notes

6 min

Scenario notes allow you to document and communicate essential details about your scenario﻿ with other Make users. Notes serve as an effective way to provide context, instructions, or explanations about different parts of your scenario﻿.

Before you start using notes, keep in mind:

* You can attach a note to any module or any route in your scenario﻿.

* Each module or route can contain up to 200 notes.

* Each note has a 10,000-character limit.

* When you use a lot of formatting, like emojis and links, you might reach the note length limit faster than without formatting.

* Make stores your notes in HTML format, which uses extra characters to set formatting.

* Scenario notes are not included when you [clone](https://help.make.com/clone-a-scenario "clone") or [share a scenario via a link](https://help.make.com/scenario-sharing "share a scenario via a link").

### Create notes

To add a note to a module or route:

1

Right-click on the module or route where you want to add a note.

2

Select **Add a note** from the context menu.

![Document image](https://images.archbee.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/sirXheD7xX_Fm9AYGFQJn-20260317-125903.png?format=webp "Document image")

﻿

3

Enter your content in the text editor.

![Document image](https://images.archbee.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/AU7EIlxcVJaLmO4s-MLP6-20260317-130302.png?format=webp "Document image")

﻿

4

Optional: Use the formatting toolbar to style your text, add links, or insert emojis.

5

Click **OK** to save your note.

This is how notes are displayed in a Scenario Builder when:

1. A note is attached to a module

2. A note is attached to a route

### View notes

You have several options to view notes in your scenario:

| What to do | What you'll see |
| --- | --- |
| Hover over the note marker | A preview of the note |
| Click once on a note marker | The complete note content with scrolling for longer notes |
| Click the sticky note icon in the toolbar | The note panel showing previews of all scenario notes |

### Edit notes

To edit a note:

1

Click once on a note marker, or hover over it and click the note.

2

Click the **three dots** in the top-right corner of the note window, and then **Edit**.

![Document image](https://images.archbee.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/gVyqsVwCJJRom0s3uLs0B-20260317-134444.png?format=webp "Document image")

﻿

You can also just double-click on a note to open it in edit mode.

3

Make your changes in the text editor.

4

Click **OK** to save your updates or **Cancel** to discard them.

The edits to your note will be saved.

### Delete notes

To delete a note:

1

Click once on a note marker, or hover over it and click the note.

2

Click the **three dots** in the top-right corner of the note window, and then **Delete**.

![Document image](https://images.archbee.com/oAyFj2GHlBeBVWF5OAir2/3L9zk3BUI351ilFu3Z0L0-20260317-133207.png?format=webp "Document image")

﻿

3

Click **Delete** once again to confirm.

The note will be deleted. This action is permanent and cannot be undone.

### Best practices

* Use notes to document complex logic or explain non-obvious workflow decisions.

* Add implementation details that might help other team members understand your approach.

* Document any specific requirements or dependencies for modules

* Explain the purpose of different routes to clarify workflow branching.

* Keep notes concise but informative for better team collaboration.
