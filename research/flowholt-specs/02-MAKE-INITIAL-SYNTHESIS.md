This file captures the first high-signal conclusions from the scraped Make corpus and the earlier Academy observation plan.

## 1. Make is not organized around the canvas alone

Repeated evidence across the corpus shows that Make treats the editor as one surface inside a broader operational platform.

Implication for FlowHolt:
- the platform must mature its control plane, not just its builder polish

## 2. Make teaches through domain hubs

The help center is structured around stable product domains:
- credits and operations
- scenarios and connections
- apps and modules
- data and mapping
- tools
- resources
- organizations and teams
- profile settings
- AI agents

Implication for FlowHolt:
- the final plan should be organized by durable product domains, not by random feature ideas

## 3. Scenario behavior is treated as an explicit runtime contract

Pages like `scenario-settings`, `scenario-execution-flow`, `scenario-execution-cycles-and-phases`, `manage-incomplete-executions`, and `overview-of-error-handling` show that runtime behavior is first-class.

Implication for FlowHolt:
- the plan must define runtime semantics in detail, including retries, incomplete runs, sequential processing, privacy, rollback, commit, cycle limits, and deactivation rules

## 4. Configuration depth is part of product maturity

Make exposes detailed settings for:
- scenarios
- modules
- connections
- user profile
- organizations
- teams
- AI agents
- billing and analytics

Implication for FlowHolt:
- the final plan must include a full settings catalog, not only major page names

## 5. AI agents are a real product system, not just a node type

The corpus shows a full AI-agent layer:
- AI agent inventory
- agent configuration
- context or knowledge uploads
- tools
- scenarios as tools
- module tools
- MCP tools
- testing and training
- trigger-specific agent workflows

Implication for FlowHolt:
- the agent plan must cover both inventory-level management and Studio-level execution

## 6. Shared assets are a maturity multiplier

Connections, credentials, variables, data stores, templates, scenario inputs and outputs, and knowledge resources are all reusable product objects.

Implication for FlowHolt:
- the final plan must define asset boundaries, ownership, scoping, discoverability, and reuse rules clearly

## 7. Collaboration is structured, not incidental

The Make corpus reinforces:
- organizations
- teams
- roles
- team-level consumption
- shared agent inventory
- shared credentials and reusable assets

Implication for FlowHolt:
- collaboration must be designed into the platform model rather than added later as a thin member list

## 8. The docs reveal planning priorities

The largest Make domains from the corpus are:
- scenario builder and authoring
- AI agents and MCP
- tenancy and governance
- integrations and connections
- runtime and observability

Implication for FlowHolt:
- the ultimate plan should prioritize these same areas because they are the highest-surface maturity drivers

## Immediate next planning outputs

The next Make-based planning steps should produce:
- a FlowHolt information architecture map
- a control-plane object model
- a Studio surface map
- an AI-agent system blueprint
- a settings catalog skeleton
- a permissions and governance skeleton
